War Impact — Commodity Overview
Commodity futures prices vs pre-war baseline (Feb 24, 2026). The conflict's biggest market impact has been on European natural gas (+85%) and fertiliser (+37%), with grains showing more moderate moves due to ample supplies from record harvests.
⚡ Energy Shock
TTF European gas +85% since war. Qatar LNG force majeure + Hormuz closure = dual supply shock. Henry Hub stable at $3.06 — US-Europe arbitrage spread at ~$15/MMBtu.
🌱 Fertiliser Crisis
Urea +37% to $665/tonne. Hormuz carries 30% of global fertiliser. Spring planting season at risk. Corn acreage projected down 6M acres. WFP: 45M more food insecure.
🌾 Grains — Moderate
Record US corn/soy harvests capping upside. "Soybean Pivot" underway as farmers avoid nitrogen-intensive corn. Wheat at 9-month high $6.42 on Mar 9, now $6.07.
🕊 Ceasefire Signal
Brent fell ~5% on Mar 25 on US 15-point ceasefire proposal. Iran denies direct talks. Goldman base case: Hormuz normalises April over 4 weeks. Markets pricing in ~30% probability.

CF Industries (US) — Winner
Using cheap domestic Henry Hub gas ($3.06) while selling at global prices. Stock up 30% in March to ~$137. 97% capacity utilisation.
Yara International (Europe) — Loser
Reliant on TTF gas at €62/MWh. Forced to cut European production by 25%. Massive cost disadvantage vs US/Russian producers.
No Strategic Reserve
Unlike oil (SPR), there is no global strategic nitrogen reserve. Once spring planting decisions are made, yields cannot be recovered mid-season.
